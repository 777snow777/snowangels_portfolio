Dodder

Spring 2025 - Present

Dodder is a localized web archive, installed on a Raspberry Pi at community events in New York City. It holds stories of climate grief through field recordings and written entries.

I designed this archive to only be accessible through physical presence, to those who have either been in direct contact with the Raspberry Pi or through devices connected to the same WiFi network as the raspberry pi. Inspired by concepts of Degrowth, it contrasts the dominant application of modern web-based technologies as means for expanding influence, and instead presents these technologies as tools for community placemaking and deep listening.

By grounding digital communication technology in physical presence, Dodder brings attention to the interconnections between these technologies and our living ecosystems. Therein, this archive is an invitation to re-relate to web technologies through a lens of socio-ecological care.

JavaScript, HTML, CSS, PostgreSQL, Raspberry Pi
